Trading Grip

The following is a checklist to make sure that you didn't look over anything
that you may have wished you had thought of before entering a trade:

Relative potential for the price to plummet? (1m candles)

Some stocks may go down the entire day but never have a big move at any given minute.
Knowing the likelihood of this is important and there are often behavioral hints.

At what level you can identify that you are wrong about the trade?

You can't know anything 100%. The best traders know it is about probability,
not predictability. Do you know ahead of time where to get out?

Time of day significance of potential price action?

Whether it's in the premarket, near the market open, later in the day or after the
4pm close, the exact same stock with same news can behave differently.

Are there warrants for this stock?

There's usually another ticker with a 'W' at the end or '/WS' sometimes such as on TDA.
If warrants exist it's a good idea to check at what price they are exercisable.

Amount of short interest

How much short interest the stock has, or any tendency of it to increase
or decrease around time intervals can be taken to mean negative things,
and if unexpectedly wrong can be positive for the stock.

Are you satisfied with your familiarity with the details of...? (+2)

Multiyear chart & vol

Always zoom out to get the bigger picture. There may be no direct correlation
between it going from $100 -> $1 over the last two years and what it will do today,
but it is important to get as many puzzle pieces in place as you can.
